Family

A mountain goat was chumping grass . Its long tounge scuttled out for more in the cervices of rocks . Its brown fur ruffled by the wind hid it in the sorroundings. It froze every now and then on hearing a stone falling or hearing an eagle cry, then started chewing again. Layers of fine crystal sparkled on the grey and brown boulders in the pale sunlight.

A shape stuck out of the mountain wall and fell on the goat. There was sound of bone cracking and tendons being torn. Fresh blood flecked the rocks and grass red.The black spots on leopardess' fur rose and fell with her fast breaths. A triumphant roar tore through the sky. The dead goat shook by the eager bites and tugging of the leopard cubs. They had come a few months before and their hunger increased with each day. Whatever the leopardess hunted down was not enough to fill their bellies. She took out the bigger organs and chewed them into smaller pieces which the cubs could swallow. Two of the cubs started fighting over a big chunk of meat, pulling it away from each other. She carried them one by one in her jaws and dropped them with the others. Then she also dug her mouth into the caracass. Afterwards the whole lot fell asleep, the hair on their muzzles dyed red .

“ This one for fifteen rupees madam.” Bhola said. “ Too costly… But I can take two for twenty.” said the woman. Bhola considered the offer for a while and agreed. His bag was filled with many crafts of wood — wooden pen fitted with cheap pearls, combs, cross- stands, little idols of gods and goddesses, key rings,little men carrying a tiny axe,women with bangles on their wrist,cars,houses– almost anything that caught his fancy he made and sold it for a living. It was beyond evening and he started for home. “ ey Bhola.” shouted a short man. He hopped to Bhola with his firm potbelly swinging from side to side. “So ..huff… made any ..huff…sold anything today?” asked the man . “ Just a little.” said Bhola in a low voice. His head was down and gaze away from the man. “When will I get my money then?May be next week or next month or may be next year.” The man was shouting. “I am trying.I am not able to sell much.” said Bhola “I have waited enough. Do what you may but I want my money before this month ends.”

Bhola knocked at the door of a shanty house. A thin woman in a yellow dress opened the door. She took the jhola from his shoulder and kept in one corner. “ Baba for you.” immediately a glass of water was thrust at his face by little hands. He drank the water slowly taking few sips at a time. Then he put down the glass and gave out a deliberate sigh.“ Now I feel refreshed.” said Bhola. The girl was fidgeting on the spot smiling with anticipation. The bangles on her hands jingled against each other. “ So, what is there for my girl today?” he said . His right hand searched inside the pockets of his kurta.” Ah! I found something.” He took out a pack of colourful sketches. The girl delightedly took hold of them admiring the bright attrative packet. “Baba I will draw and show you somthing.” she said happily and ran to fetch her drawing book.

“ How was today?” asked his wife. “Fine” said Bhola. She went to the corner and picked up the jhola. “ It's been just as heavy for many days.” she said wieghng it in her arms. Bhola knew he had to put up some fight. “ Okay. The sales have not been that good for some time. But from now on things will only improve. Who does not have a bad day or two?” said Bhola. His wife knew he was lying. It had not been just two bad days. She sat besides him . The furrows in her forehead dissappeared and her expression became soft.Bhola was looking outside through the plastic door by which ran a small stream of sewage and waste. ”For how long we will go on like this ?” she said.

Bhola was unable to sleep even though he was tired. He was sitting on the stool watching the sleeping figures of his wife and daughter. His wife looked much younger without the lines of worry which she bore during the day. His daughter was curled against her mother. Neon light fell from the gaps in the plastic wall painting the room black and orange. On the roof the mice squeaked and scuttled. In one corner lay a water can almost empty. His wife would have to line up in the morning before the tap. His daughter would have to help in chores too. A stink of sewage came wafting over the hot night air . A pipe must have burst somewhere. Bhola thought how could he make their lives better. He would put in more hours in selling and roaming the streets. He would make new things something that would catch the eye of people with money. May be he should make a big statue. That would be difficult and expensive. And if no one bought it then.. Slowly the thoughts ebbed from his mind and he fell asleep.

Coal black rocks sprawled over the wall of the cave . The lepardess had not hunted for a week now. The prey was becoming harder to find. The one she had spotted and chased springing from rock to rock, and finally caught hold of , slipped from her jaws and jumped into the cold blue stream . Since the past two days snow rained continuously from the dense white sky. A row of small sleeping heads with their bodies mushed together lay huddled in a corner. The howling of the wind reverberated inside the cave. The leopardess sat on all fours with her long furry tail curved agianst her body. Her grey eyes shone from the light coming out of the mouth of the cave which looked like a white window inside the dark cave. Sometimes her neck fell to one side and she fell asleep. But the howling wind kept her awake. Earlier the biggest of the cubs held the neck of the other one in its jaws while third was pulling at his tail by its canines. The big cub squeaked and meowed and let go of its hold . Then it turned towards the other and growled at him. Both of them then stood on their hind legs and lapped their paws against each other. Meanwhile the third one got on top of a big rock and jumped in between them. The bigger one saw its chance in the confusion and kicked one of them and bit the other at the back. Then it growled and meowed in victory. The big cub felt emboldened. It sat carefully on all its fours. Its lower body just inches above the ground and its thick tail stretched out and started taking small tapping steps towards the mouth of the cave. When it reached near enough, the leopardess gave out a low runting snarl. It quickly shot as a bolt from there and cuddled in between the other cubs.

On the table lay various chisels, gouges, knives and a pair of mallets. Coils of shaven wood lay everywhere on the floor. Here and there lay unfinished pieces of men, women, horse ,cattle, boat and others which could not be yet made out. Paint brushes of many lengths were dipped inside small tin cans filled with varnish or polish. The smell of turpentine and paint floated in air. Regular knocking of wood against wood rattled across the room.Bhola was chipping at a new piece and chiseled till a face, eyes , nose and limbs appeared on the figuriene . Then he rubbed the surface with sand paper and applied paint and varnish. His daughter was standing nearby holding a drawing chart. She had drawn a mountain with river flowing straight from it. The half sun shone with straight rays and rested in between the slopes of mountains. Clouds and leaves of trees had the same shape and differed only in color. By the river stood a hut drawn with a skewed slanting roof. It was not wise to disturb Bhola when he was deep in woodcraft. 'Ba…Ba..' she began in her thin voice. Bhola had seen her coming from the side. He turned towards her, his face still locked in concentration broke into a smile. “ Show me.” he said taking the paper into his rough hands. “ Its good. Hmm.. the mountains are perfect , the sun is also drawn well. The trees are ok. But the roof could have been drawn better. Overall it's a good drawing in the first attempt.” came Bhola's verdict. The girl was not too happy with such comments. “ Even I could not make good pieces when I began. I used to get badly thrashed by my baba. I was slow and only learned after a lot of practice.” The girl was smug over the thought of her father being lousy and getting beaten for it.

Just then his wife came over breathing heavily carrying a big can of water. Drops of sweat broke on her face and ran down till her chin. Her wet hair was plastered on her skull. She sat on the stool and her look lingered on the wooden pieces. She shook her head hopelessely. This was not lost on Bhola. “ Where are you going now?” she asked. “ I have to meet someone.” Bhola said as he started to go. The girl hurriedly folded her drawing and hid it under the mat. “ Baba I want to come too.” Both of them went outside. She saw the wood litter spread across the room. “ Now I have to clean up this mess too.” she thought. A spasm of anger ran through her . She got up and with a fling of her arm knocked down the tools and wooden pieces . She was on the verge of upturning the table when one of the pieces caught her eye. Curious she picked it up from the floor. It was not one of those pieces which looked all the same to each other. There was a small mole on its cheek, one of its big toe was missing and its thin body was painted over as a yellow sari. It was her figuriene.

Clouds had broken against the blue sky. The leopardess was waiting patiently above the grey rocks, her eyes set on a patch of vegetation growing on flat rock jutting out of the mountain. The rossetted patterns on her coat blended in with the rocks rendering her invisible. At one time she was tempted to pounce on the eagle which flew right in front of her. But that would have been hardly sufficient for a family for four and she could have fallen into the cold river below.

The smallest of pebbles fell making ticking sounds against the slaty rocks. From above a big mountain goat was trudging down breaking its fall at the edges and gaps in the mountain . The moment its hoofs touched the grass patch the leopardess moved.The blades of her shoulder slid smoothly under her furry skin,with her tail floating behind. Her jaw was opened in mid air as she pounced on the goat. She missed its neck and instead her jaw cut into the goat's flank. The powerful goat kicked its hindlegs everywhere in the air. The long tail of the leopardess danced as she struggled to keep her hold. She had to swerve continuously to avoid getting a kick in her guts or a blow of the strong horns aimed at her chest .

The cubs meowed and jumped up and down all about her, sniffing and scratching at her dusty and ruffled coat with their small paws . She growled with all her canines showing and they all fell back quietly. She walked limply towards a corner and lay down on one side of her flank. Blood was trickling down from above her hind leg cut open by a wound. Her breath came out in broken grunts. Her neck shuddered with pain as she turned to lick her wound but it would not reach. In defeat she put her head down on the pebbled floor. She was exhausted and famished. Then she felt soft clicking of tounges against her torn flesh. One of her cubs came and licked her face and nose and the lenght of her furry neck. This time she did not growl at them. The scowls of pain on her face were melting . She was looking outside the cave , a window of clear blue sky and white mountains shining in her eyes.

“Where you left your Baba?” asked Bhola's wife . Her daughter had returned back alone. “Baba met someone on our way back. Then baba told me you go home I will come afterwards.”

It was late in the night. Bhola had not yet come. Bhola's wife was pacing from wall to wall and immediately coming out on hearing the slightest of noise. Her daughter had refused to sleep till Bhola did not come but she couldn't keep up and fell asleep. The plastic board which served as the door was slowly pushed open and Bhola staggered in. His clothes were torn at places and his left eye was puffy and black. His hair was messed all over , blood was oozing out from a deep cut below his chin . There were a few scratches on his face.

“What happened to you?”cried his wife out aloud. His lips fumbled silently. She supported his back by holding across his sholuders and helped him into the bed. His back was covered with foot prints stamped all over. He kept on mumbling something till he fell unconscious.

It was in afternoon when Bhola woke up. As he tried to get up pain prickled all over his muscles and bones . His wounds were clean and dressed. His daughter was sitting besides bent over her drawing book. “Amma Baba got up.” shouted his daughter. His wife appeared from the kitchen corner . “How do you feel now?” said his wife. Her unkempt hair fell across her back, her eyes were puffed from waking all night , her stained cheeks were glistening with fresh tears and new lines had crept over her face. Bhola felt the beats of his heart slowing down. A terrible anguish welled up within him as he looked at her pained face. . “I am all right.It will be fine.” he wanted to say but words had left him.

“Here see my new drawing Baba”. His daughter held up the drawing to his face. It was the sketch of mountains, river and a hut. “Look I got the roof right this time. Does my drawing look nice now?” she said . Her face was beaming with pride. The numbness within him broke into a smile of sadness. “ It's beautiful now.” he said.
